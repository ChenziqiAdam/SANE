01 Capabilities

A quiet librarian for the thoughts you've already written.

Six small powers that, together, turn a folder of notes into something that remembers itself.

→ 01

Semantic similarity, not keyword soup.

Notes are compared by meaning using embeddings. A piece on "neural networks" finds your old jottings on "backpropagation" — even when no word matches.

→ 02

Enrichment via frontmatter.

Tags, keywords, links, summaries — written to the YAML at the top, never into the body of your note.

→ 03

Triggers, your way.

Instantly on save, after a delay, on a schedule, or strictly manual.

→ 04

Five providers, one plugin.

OpenAI, Google AI, Grok, Azure, or fully local LLMs via Ollama.

→ 05

Costs, capped.

Daily budget limits and real-time cost tracking. No surprises.

→ 06

Always reversible.

Every field SANE adds is prefixed with sane_. One command undoes everything — across a single note or the whole vault.

→ 07

Scoped to a folder.

Point SANE at a single folder. Everything else stays untouched, unread, unembedded.

02 Process

Four movements, repeated quietly, every time you write.

i.

You write.

Add or edit a note. SANE notices, waits a beat (or fires immediately — your choice).

ii.

It listens.

An embedding is computed for the new content and compared against your vault's semantic map.

iii.

It finds kin.

The most relevant existing notes surface as context — the threads worth weaving.

iv.

It enriches.

Tags, keywords, links, and a summary land in the frontmatter. Your prose stays exactly as you left it.

03 What it leaves behind

A handful of quiet annotations.

All additions live in the YAML frontmatter, prefixed with sane_. Your note's body is sacred. Delete the prefix, and every trace of SANE is gone.

neural-networks.md 
# your original frontmatter, untouched
title: "On Neural Networks"
created_at: "2025/5/5"

# what SANE quietly added
sane_tags: ["machine-learning", "neural-networks"]
sane_keywords: ["backpropagation", "training data"]
sane_links: ["Introduction to AI", "Neural Net Basics"]
sane_summary: "A guide to neural networks and their applications."
sane_version: "1.0"
